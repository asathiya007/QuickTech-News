Samsung Foldables - The Future of Mobile?

Akshay Sathiya - February 20th, 2019

Summary

  • In the ever so quickly growing market of mobile devices, such as smartphones, tablets, etc. comes a new revolution in design: foldability. Foldables are are mobile devices that, exactly as the name implies, can be folded. One of the biggest companies to display their design of a foldable mobile device is Samsung.
  • Samsung revealed a prototype of the Galaxy Fold, Samsung's new foldable mobile device, at one of their recent developer conferences. The big reveal of the prototype first showed off its foldable design, which appeared much smoother and resembled non-foldable devices to a much greater than degree than other foldable device prototypes, which clearly marks progress in the design and development of foldable mobile devices, not just by Samsung, but by the tech industry as a whole. The Galaxy Fold is set to launch on April 26.
  • Other notable features of the Galaxy Fold prototype include but are not limited to the remarkable amount of storage for a mobile device (12 GB RAM, 512 GB of storage) six cameras, and a size change from 4.6 inches (folded) to 7.3 inches (unfolded). The Galaxy Fold was built to provide an exceptional amount of functionality and capability to the user and their various needs, whether it be work, entertainment, design, etc.

Implications

  • This may signal the start of a new trend: foldable mobile devices. For years and years, people have sought smaller devices but bigger screens. This just may be the perfect solution to such a desire. The foldability of the Galaxy Fold and other foldable devices allows it to be compact when stored in one's pocket as well as large when expanded for either professional or entertainment use.
  • This is officially Samsung's stride into the foldable mobile device market. The prospects of foldable mobile devices has been questioned ever since the concept of a folding mobile device was thought of. The entry of a large firm in the tech industry, Samsung, will most likely spur other large companies, such as Apple, Huawei, etc. to start building their own foldable mobile devices.
  • This may be the next step of a phenomenon I like to call design convergence: when the functionality of multiple devices is combined into one. One such example of this is with today's smartphones, which combine the functionality (to some extent) of a game console (mobile gaming), the telephone (calling people), and music players (to listen/play music). The advent of foldable mobile devices may present something even more grand: a fusion between a smartphone and a laptop. For most people, their laptops are work devices and their smartphones are play/entertainment devices, but with foldable mobile devices, it is very possible that mobile devices can continue to be the professional and entertainment devices they have been but now to a greater degree, since a larger mobile device with more storage and computing power can likely perform tasks that were initially reserved for computers. This marks a potential change in lifestyle and productivity, since the roles of two very powerful devices, are slowly and may one day fully converge toward one device: the foldable.
